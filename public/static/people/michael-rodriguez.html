Biography

Michael focuses on cryptographic protocols for IoT and blockchain consensus mechanisms. He completed his PhD in Applied Cryptography at Stanford in 2022.

His research interests span across several critical areas in modern cryptography, with particular emphasis on developing practical solutions for resource-constrained environments. Michael's work has been instrumental in advancing the understanding of consensus mechanisms in permissionless blockchain networks.

Research Focus

Michael's current research focuses on designing efficient cryptographic protocols that can operate in IoT environments while maintaining strong security guarantees. He is particularly interested in zero-knowledge proof systems and their applications to privacy-preserving blockchain technologies.
